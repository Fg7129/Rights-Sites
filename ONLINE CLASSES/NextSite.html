DEPRESSION(MENTAL ILLNESSES)

Depression is a mental health condition that affects a person's mood, thoughts, and physical well-being. It's more than just feeling "down" for a few days; it can persist for weeks, months, or even longer, making everyday tasks feel overwhelming.

Common signs and feelings people with depression might experience include:

  1. Persistent sadness or emptiness.
  2. Loss of interest.
  3. Fatigue or lack of energy.
  4. Difficulty concentrating.
  5. Physical symptoms.
  6. Thoughts of suicide

TYPES OF DEPRESSION:

There are many types of it, so I will tell you the main types of depression:

  1. Major Depressive Disorder(MDD)

    This is characterized by a persistent feeling of sadness or loss of interest in most daily activities, lasting at least 2 weeks.

  2. Dystymia

    This is a chronic form of depression with symptoms that are less severe but las for at least 2 years.

  3. Bipolar Disorder.

    It involves alternating periods of depression and mania or hypomania.

  4. Seasonal Affective Disorder.

    A type of depression that occurs seasonally most commonly during less sunny months.

  5. Postpartum Depression

    This occurs after childbirth.

  6. Psychotic Depression

    This is a severe form of depression that is accompanied by psychosis such as delusions or hallucinations

  7. Atypical Depression

    Atypical depression is a subtype of major depression characterized by specific features like temporary mood improvement in response to positive events (called "mood reactivity") increased sleep and appetite ,weight gain and heavy limbs.It's considered atypical because it doesn't fit the typical pattern of depression
